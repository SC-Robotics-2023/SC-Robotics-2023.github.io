About The Team

Saddleback College Robotics is an undergraduate student team that competes in the University Rover Challenge hosted by Mars Society. SC Robotics allows engineering and scientist students to work on complex systems within a multidisciplinary team to build the next generation of Mars rovers that will explore the Red Planet. The systems are designed using professional modeling and simulation software, and built using innovative manufacturing and fabrication solutions.

Subsystems

Arm

Engineers are responsible for the design and manipulation of a robotics arm with an end-effector by implementing inverse kinematics and communicating with the Chassis and System Subsystems for integration.

Drive

Engineers are responsible for the design and simulation of a robust suspension system, engineer wheels with traction and crushability, plan the powertrain of the Subsystem, and write code. Additionally, engineers will communicate with the Chassis and System Substems for integration.

Chassis

Engineers are responsible for the design of the chassis, planning electrical layout with power distribution and protection circuits, designing housing/mounts for electronics, setting up the communication system between the rover and the drive station, and building the drive station for the operator. Additionally, engineers will communicate with the System Substem for integration.

Science

Scientists are responsible for designing and testing experiments to detect life on Mars. At the competition site, scientists will analyze data and answer questions from judges regarding our experiments and past missions on Mars. 

Engineers are responsible for integrating designs to detect life on Mars using appropriate sensors, and circuits. Engineers will also communicate with the System and the Chassis Subsystem for integration.

Autonomy

Engineers are responsible for writing Autonomy code that will interface with necessary cameras, GPS and sensors. Engineers will also communicate with the Chassis and System Subsystems for integration.

Systems

Members/Engineers/Scientists are responsible for facilitating integration across Subsystems, understanding the Rover System in its entirety, and managing purchasing and timeline.

Business

Members are responsible for fundraising, arranging presentations, preparing and coordinating outreach events, taking photos and videos during working hours, and directing the production of the SAR video. 

University Rover Challenge

The University Rover Challenge (URC) is a yearly international engineering and science competition hosted by the Mars Society. Students design and build a mars rover with a robotic arm, on-board life detection experiment, and autonomous navigation system. The competition culminates in the URC Finals, where the top teams travel to the Mars Desert Research Station near Hanksville, Utah to participate in four missions: Extreme Retrieval, Equipment Servicing, Science, and Autonomy.

Extreme Retrieval

Traversal of the hilly, rocky, and sandy terrain to deliver tools, materials, and minerals of interest to astronauts over a distance of up to 1 km.

Equipment Servicing

Use of the robotic arm to load a payload into a spaceship and prepare it for launch. Tasks include flipping switches, typing on a keyboard, manipulating a joystick, and inserting a USB drive into a port.

Science Mission

In-field analysis of soil and mineral samples to determine the presence of extant or extinct life using on-rover experiments. Science members present findings to competition judges after the analysis period.

Autonomy Mission

Autonomous navigation of increasingly challenging terrain to reach checkpoints and travel through marked gates. Checkpoints come in the form of visible ARUCO markers or GPS coordinates.
